# Workshop 1 Facilitator Guide (75 min)

**Title:** Technology = Tools Made by People  
**Theme:** Nothing is magic; technology is made by people on purpose.  
**Audience:** Families with kids ~4–10  
**Format:** Parent + child required (no drop-off). Minimal screens. No open internet. No kid accounts.

---

## 1) Purpose & outcomes

### Big idea (kid-safe)
**Technology is a tool made by people to do a job.**

### What kids should be able to do by the end
- Say (in their own words): “Technology is a tool made by people for a job.”
- Name 3–5 everyday technologies and what job each one does.
- Understand: tools aren’t “good or bad” by default; **how we use them** matters.

### What parents should be able to do by the end
- Use a calm, values-first frame (not shame/fear).
- Ask two questions at home:
  1) **“What job is this tool doing for us?”**
  2) **“Is it doing that job well?”**
- Leave with a completed **Family Tech Values Card**.

---

## 2) Facilitator mindset

### Tone
- **Warm, calm, practical.**
- Avoid: “screens are evil,” “kids today,” or fear-based stories.

### Primary facilitation goals
- Keep parents + kids **talking to each other**, not only to you.
- Keep the room **moving** (short instruction → activity → share-out → move on).
- Normalize struggle without letting it take over.

### Redirection script (when a parent starts venting)
> “That sounds hard—and you’re not alone. Let’s bring it back to our two questions: what job is the tool doing, and what boundary would help it do that job better?”

---

## 3) Materials & prep

### Printables
1) **Tool / Toy / Both** cards (1 set per family)
   - Print on cardstock if possible.
   - If you don’t have cards, substitute real objects (see list below).
2) **Family Tech Values Card** handout (1 per family + 2 extras)
3) Optional: “Vocabulary mini-sheet” (Tool / Job / Choice / Interface)

### Supplies
- Sticky notes (1 pad per 2 families)
- Markers (thick, kid-friendly)
- Pens (for parents)
- Painter’s tape (for posting charts)
- Timer (phone is fine; keep it discreet)
- Poster paper or a whiteboard + dry erase markers

### Optional objects (backup instead of cards)
- TV remote
- Flashlight
- Measuring cup
- Bicycle helmet
- Stuffed animal
- Toy car
- Book
- Phone (facilitator only; keep face down)

### Room setup (arrive 30–40 min early)
- Tables: ideally **families seated together** (2–3 families per table).
- A “front” wall/board for your big idea and prompts.
- Handouts placed at each table:
  - 1 Family Tech Values Card
  - 1 set of Tool/Toy/Both cards (or 10–15 objects per table station)
- A simple sign at the door: “Parent + child workshop (no drop-off)”

### Board plan (write before people arrive)
1) Header: **Workshop 1: Technology = Tools Made by People**
2) Big idea: **Tech = tool + made by people + has a job**
3) Two family questions:
   - What job is it doing?
   - Is it doing that job well?
4) Norms:
   - Kind
   - Curious
   - No shaming

---

## 4) Safety / boundaries (say this plainly)
- Kids stay with their adult.
- No photographing other families without permission.
- Minimal screens: no free device time during workshop.

**If a child becomes dysregulated:**
> “Totally okay to take a hallway break. Come back when you’re ready.”

---

## 5) Run of show (minute-by-minute)

### 0–5 min — Welcome + norms
**Goal:** set expectations + safety.

**Facilitator script (tight):**
> “Welcome. This workshop is parent + child together—this is not daycare. We’ll do short instructions and then you’ll talk and build as a family.”
> 
> “Our norms: be kind, be curious, and no shaming—every family is figuring this out.”
> 
> “Today’s big idea: technology is tools made by people to do a job. Tools should serve the family; screens don’t run the home.”

**Quick connection prompt (hands up):**
- “Raise your hand if you’ve ever argued about screens in the last week.”
  - Laugh gently. Normalize.

### 5–15 min — Concept story: tools do jobs
**Goal:** give kids a usable definition + prime parents.

**Teach (2 minutes):**
- “A tool is something people make to help do a job.”
- “Technology is just tools—some are digital, some are not.”

**Call-and-response (kid-friendly):**
- Facilitator: “Technology is…”
- Kids: “A tool!”
- Facilitator: “Made by…”
- Kids: “People!”
- Facilitator: “To do a…”
- Kids: “Job!”

**Family prompt (3 minutes):**
> “Turn to your adult/kid: name three tools you used today. What job did each do?”

**Introduce “interface” lightly (optional, 1 minute):**
- “Interface means the ‘handle’—buttons/menus—that tell you how to use the tool.”

**Share-out (3–4 minutes):**
- Take 3 quick examples. Keep it moving.

### 15–40 min — Activity 1: Tool / Toy / Both sorting
**Goal:** families practice “job” thinking; you observe and coach.

**Instructions (2 minutes):**
> “At your table, sort the cards into three piles: Tool, Toy, Both. There are no perfect answers—your job is to explain your thinking.”

**Work time (10–12 minutes):**
- Walk around.
- Ask questions that force “job” thinking:
  - “What job does that do?”
  - “Who is it for?”
  - “When does it become too much?”

**Mini-share (8–10 minutes):**
Pick 3–4 families to share one interesting card each.

**Facilitator prompts if they get stuck:**
- “If it’s mostly for fun, it leans Toy.”
- “If it helps with a task, it leans Tool.”
- “If it can do both, put it in Both—and name the jobs.”

**Older kid extension (optional):**
- “Pick 2 cards and write one sentence: ‘This tool helps with ___.’”

### 40–55 min — Activity 2: “Who made this?” chain
**Goal:** shift blame from child to design choices; build agency.

**Set-up (1 minute):**
> “Next idea: tools are made by people. That means buttons, autoplay, and notifications are choices.”

**Choose 2–3 examples (prep these in advance):**
- YouTube, iPad/game console, Alexa, TV remote, streaming app.

**Facilitator-led chain (10 minutes):**
On the board, draw a simple chain:

**Company → Designers/Engineers → Interface choices → Your feelings/behavior**

Script:
> “An app is made by a company. Companies hire designers and engineers. Those people decide what buttons exist and what happens next.”
> 
> “So if something feels pushy, that might be a design choice—not your kid being ‘bad.’”

**Family prompt (3–4 minutes):**
> “Pick one tool your family uses. What do you think it’s trying to get you to do next?”

**Quick shares (2 minutes):**
- 2 families.

### 55–70 min — Family deliverable: Tech Values Card
**Goal:** leave with a tangible plan.

**Set-up (1 minute):**
> “Now we’ll make your fridge card. Keep it simple. The goal is something you’ll actually use once this week.”

**Values Card fields (write on board):**
- Our family uses tech for (pick **3**)
- Our red lines (pick **3**)
- Our default rule

**Work time (8–10 minutes):**
Walk around and help families keep it **specific**.

**Examples (read out loud as inspiration):**
- Values: learning, creativity, connection, music, rest
- Red lines: no devices in bedrooms; no secret accounts; no autoplay on weekdays; no YouTube without a parent; devices parked during meals
- Default rule: “Ask a parent before new apps / new accounts.”

**Share-out (optional, 2 minutes):**
- Ask for one “red line” idea to share.

### 70–75 min — Close + “Family Move”
**Goal:** 1 action this week.

**Script:**
> “Your Family Move for the week: put this card somewhere visible and use it one time—just once—when a tech decision comes up.”
> 
> “Next week: systems and automation—how ‘smart’ can come from simple steps.”

**Exit line:**
> “Thanks for showing up. The win is talking about it together.”

## 6) Common scenarios & how to handle

### A) Parent says: “My kid is addicted / it’s hopeless.”
> “It feels that way sometimes. Let’s pick one tool and one job, and choose one boundary that helps it do that job better.”

### B) Kid is shy / won’t talk
- Give them a role:
  - “You’re the ‘job detective.’ Point to the card and tell us what job it does.”

### C) Siblings present
- Make it explicit:
  - “If you brought siblings, that’s okay—just keep one adult paired to one ‘primary kid’ during activities.”
